As with many ideas, it was reading something not-too-recently and a suggestion from a friend that sparked this project off. A customisable homepage that doesn't use the server for anything aside from the markup and javascript to make it happen.

Of course reality is never quite so easy, unfortunately not every site has the most modern web technology deployed - so for certain widgets the app does still have to rely on the server, where possible trying to limit this… and provide alternatives.

Status

AptoDomus is in alpha, which means new features will be added and changes may occur. Though I feel like it is now at a stage where the basics are there and the public can begin using it.

The Name

AptoDomus is made up of two latin words: Apto, meaning adjustable and Domus, meaning home. Plus it seems to ring nicely.

Privacy

This site uses localStorage, an allocated amount of space assigned to your browser on your device. It is not visible to other users on other computers; as such if you clear the correct browser cache any customisations may be lost.

The only information I will ever glean from your usage of this site is what is necessary for you to visit it, your IP address and other related information that your browser may send.

Some features may require more interaction with the local server, where this is the case the widgets will be marked with an asterisk. Primarily this involves RSS or XML feeds, if a JSON alternative is not made available.

JavaScript and Data

JavaScript is used in this instance to provide the interactivity, allowing you to customise your layout and add things to your lists; no data at any point (unless specified otherwise in the documentation) is sent back to the server… I may in the future add features that may temporarily store information or require handling of data, but I fully intend to avoid it as much as possible; the features would only be for your own end use, and provide warning of this.
