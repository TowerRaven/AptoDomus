About this WebApp

  • Return to App Documentation

It came about as I'd read about HTML5 storage and was already working on web apps that could be used locally (in an Intranet). It seemed a natural fit to work on something that would naturally allow me to actually use local storage but be on an Internet site (rather than local), but still maintain some of those benefits. With the help of a friend identifying that there was a need for a decent solution; it became an obvious choice for a pet project.

Privacy

In an intranet environment, unless exposed in a certain manner is somewhat more secure than an internet site; but with local storage all the data is kept within the user's system, linked to their browser, that does introduce some other problems but as such I don't need to worry about handling your data beyond providing a nice interface for you to use.

The only information I will ever glean from your usage of this site is what is necessary for you to visit it, your IP address and other related information that your browser may send (check out browser fingerprinting for instance).

JavaScript and Data

JavaScript is used in this instance to provide the interactivity, allowing you to customise your layout and add things to your lists; no data at any point (unless specified otherwise in the Documentation) is sent back to the server… I may in the future add features that may temporarily store information or require handling of data, but I fully intend to avoid it as much as possible; the features would only be for your own end use, and provide warning of this.
